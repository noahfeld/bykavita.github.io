OVERVIEW

Through my production company Big Cow Studio, I directed and co-produced Healthy Gamer’s “Dr.K’s Guides,” a premium educational series with 5 (and counting) modules - over 400 episodes total - on Depression, Anxiety, Meditation, ADHD, and Trauma. My work was two fold: directing and producing the content itself, as well as developing and directing successful scripted trailers for the Guides that garnered over 100K sold copies and furthered their brand storytelling.

BRIEF

Healthy Gamer was ready to launch their first ever paid content, a coherent masterclass by Dr.K that covers the most common mental health issues we face today. As an authority on clinical and holistic mental health for the internet generation, they required an elevated, sophisticated production that was still grounded in their grassroots, built by gamers for gamers brand identity that has gained them the trust of millions worldwide.

CREATIVE STRATEGY

CONTENT
  • Education & Retention Forward: The priority is to create a platform and content that actually works to educate. iPad chalk-boarding, graphics and animations, on screen vocabulary terms and glossary, and checklists help the viewer learn alongside Dr.K.
  • Think Zen: Lean into Healthy Gamer’s Eastern foundations with calming, cozy frames, which lend to a relaxed learning environment that one can easily spend hours in.
  • Differentiate Healthy Gamer from Dr.K (the face of the company): Use a self-aware tone to retain the dad humor the internet knows and loves him for while also putting distance between the company and Dr. K for longevity’s sake.

CREATIVE STRATEGY

TRAILERS
  • Respect the Audience: Gamers have a strong BS-detector and HG is known for their transparency. All promotional content should anchor into this by being straightforward and honest.
  • Utilize Dr. K’s charm: Dr. K has built a following by approaching mental health in honest, hilarious, and often meme-able ways. Root advertising in his signature humor.
  • Main point: Dr. K always had a hard time reading sales pitches. And stitching together a trailer from the guides took way too long. So, in the trauma module trailer, we lean into his inability to sell himself, and keep it light and funny, and let the CEO and also his wife pitch the module for him. Not only does this counteract the heavy subject matter, it crucially keeps it in the language of their audience - who also hate being sold something, hate corporate music, and generally hate “the system” and don’t want to profited off of.. It performed tremendously well, and funny trailers became the new go-to for any adverts. This idea was conceived and executed by me.
